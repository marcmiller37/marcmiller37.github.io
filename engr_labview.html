Case Structures

These are labviews 'if' statements. They allow you to run code only 'if' something else is true or false. Basically, anytime you make a decision, you will need one of these.

Local Variables

Wiring can be difficult to understand and even more difficult to follow if you are not using local variables. Local variables allow you to wire more clearly and make your information readable. It can also be used to do things that are near impossible otherwise.

Wiring a Digital Input

Inputting data means sending data into the computer. Digital inputs send a value of 0 or 1 (off or on) to a computer. This on/off (1/0) can also be assumed to be a true or false statement. You can send this data through switches and other devices in order to control things within your program.

  • Practice Problems

Wiring a Digital Output

Similarly to digital inputs, digital outputs consist of a signal on/off or true/false. You can use this outputs to turn on and off motors and other devices that accept electrical signals.

Wiring a Analog Input

Analog inputs differ from digital signals because they do not just return a value of 0 or 1. They can return floats of random amounts. This allows you to use a device to measure voltage and convert those voltage measurements into other values.
